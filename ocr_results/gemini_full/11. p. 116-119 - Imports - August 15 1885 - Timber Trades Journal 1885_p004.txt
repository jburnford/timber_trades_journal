THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for the full amount.]
H. STEVENS.-In this matter, in which the debtor is a builders' merchant, of Eastbourne, the sitting for the public examination was he'd at the County Hall, Lewes, on Friday, before the Registrar (Mr. M. K. Blaker).
Mr. H. Prince, on behalf of the Official Receiver, applied for an adjournment, as some accounts for which the debtor was applied to had not been filed till the previous evening, and the Official Receiver had not had sufficient time to investigate the same.
Mr. Isaac Vinall, on behalf of the debtor, said that the particulars were duly rendered to the accountant instructed by the Receiver to prepare the accounts, who, it appeared, had not forwarded them till last night.
No delay had occurred on the part of the debtor, and, whilst not objecting to the adjournment, he asked that it might be taken as early as possible, for a composition had been unanimously accepted and that could not be confirmed till the public examination was closed.
The matter was then adjourned till the 17th inst.
