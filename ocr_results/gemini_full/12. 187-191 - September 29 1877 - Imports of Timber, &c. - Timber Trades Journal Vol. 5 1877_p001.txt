CARDIFF.
(From Messrs. Tellefsen, Wills, & Co.'s Circular, Sept. 15th.)
As reported in our last issue, stocks of pitwood and mining timber are not heavy, but prices remain unaltered, on account of the steady arrival of wood in quantities sufficient to meet the requirements of purchasers.
We see no immediate prospect of any advance in prices, unless we should experience stormy weather, which might interfere with arrivals, and thus create a temporary scarcity.
