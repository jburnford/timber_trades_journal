ELECTRICAL SUPPLIES.
The Woodhouse & Rawson Electrical Supply Co. of Gt. Britain, Limited, 11, Queen Victoria Street, London, E.C., have issued a copy of their electrical appliances, a copy of which they have kindly forwarded to us. We now find it to contain a great deal of interest upon the subject of electricity and the various appliances now employed in subjecting this potential force to the many uses of man. It has yet been found capable, and what its future we have no doubt will be largely extended. In this country vested interests militates very strongly against the wide application which the electric light has had in other countries; still, there can be no question that it is making, in spite of this, great headway, notably in saw-mills. We are pleased to see this, for the benefits of the saw-millers are without this means of illumination, whilst it is also extensively employed in the United States, and indeed in all our colonies. Those who may be contemplating the use of the electric light in saw-mills in this country cannot, we think, do better than place themselves into communication with the company we have mentioned. The catalogue referred to embraces every requisite for this purpose, besides a variety of articles applicable in buildings of all kinds, and it can be had from the company, post free for a shilling.
