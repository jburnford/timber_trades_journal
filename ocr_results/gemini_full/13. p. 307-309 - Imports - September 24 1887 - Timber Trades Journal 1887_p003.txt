THE RIGHT "MR. JOHN MORGAN."-An amusing discussion at a recent meeting of the Aberystwith Town Council, arising out of a notification from the Lord Chancellor that he had placed "John Morgan" on the Commission of the Peace, was reported in last week's Journal.
There being several gentlemen of that name, it will be remembered that the Council were in doubt who was meant. The Town Clerk has since received a letter from the Lord Chancellor, explaining that the gentleman honoured is "Mr. John Morgan, timber merchant." We congratulate Mr. Morgan on the honour conferred on him. At the meeting of the Council at which the announcement was made, the Mayor asked the Town Clerk was asked if it was not usual to first submit for the approval of the Town Council the names of gentlemen proposed to be put on the commission, and he replied that it was not done in this instance. Mr. William Thomas: Up to the very last minute the public clung to the idea that it was Mr. John Morgan, newspaper editor. I have seen votes canvassed for magistrates in this room, and has nothing to do with it. I have seen votes canvassed for magistrates in this room, and was offered half-a-crown for mine. Mr. Palmer: That was bribery and corruption. You don't mean to say that a Town Councillor can be bought for half-a-crown? I am glad I was not then a member of the Town Council.
