CARDIFF.
(From Messrs. Tellefesen, Wills, and Co.'s Circular, Oct. 15th.)
We have no change to report. The quantities arriving are quite equal to the demand, and there is no appearance at present of the high figures realized last year. At the present moment they are some eighteen pence per ton lower than in October, 1876. Norwegian and Swedish arrivals of mining timber have not been heavy, but on account of the depression in Staffordshire the demand is not good, and shippers do not find it remunerative to send wood here at the prices now obtainable.
