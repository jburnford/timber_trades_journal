On completion of the programme Mr. D. J. Morgan addressed those present, and remarked upon the pleasure he experienced in being of assistance to the club, and he endeavoured to impress upon the minds of his hearers the necessity of acquiring the art of swimming. He then presented the prizes, with appropriate and encouraging observations, to the successful competitors.
J. Griffin, Esq., the Secretary of the company, then proposed a vote of thanks to Mr. Morgan for his kindness in taking so great and continued an interest in this and all matters calculated to tend to the welfare of the employes of the dock company. The vote was carried with acclamation, as was also a vote of thanks to Mr. Griffin and Mr. Brownfield, the Superintendent (who was the originator of the club), for the help and support which the club had received from them. The proceedings then terminated.
