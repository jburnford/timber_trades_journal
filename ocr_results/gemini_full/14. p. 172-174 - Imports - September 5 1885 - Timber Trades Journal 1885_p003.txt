THE ROYAL COMMISSION ON THE STATE OF TRADE.—Certain trades in the metropolis are endeavouring to obtain a direct representation on the Royal Commission. The initiative appears to have been taken by the sawyers, carpenters, and joiners, and others in the timber trade, who are seriously affected by the importation of foreign sawn and dressed timber, house-frames, fittings, &c. It is plain that the importation affects the home trade, and a meeting of the trades has just been forced to consider the steps to be taken to secure direct representation. It will undoubtedly be a great gain to the Commission if his proposals are carried into effect, and the industries claiming to be heard are represented. We are surprised to hear that the building trade is so depressed in London, and we should not be surprised if it will seek direct representation on the Royal Commission.
