THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
It must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for the full amount.
JAMES DAY.—The meeting of the creditors of Mr. James Day, timber merchant, of Andover, was held at the Star Hotel, Andover, on the 2nd inst. The statement of affairs showed liabilities to the amount of £610 5s. 4d., the whole of which was expected to rank against the estate. The assets included stock-in-trade of Batchelor’s Barn, Andover, £50 ; book debts, £68 5s. 10d., bill of exchange £7, due in June; household furniture estimated at £70; other property, £297 10s. In consequence of the debtor making no offer, he was adjudicated a bankrupt. Mr. W. W. Slade, of Lorne Road, Havelock Park, Southsea, commercial traveller to Messrs. J. Allen & Son, was appointed trustee, the remuneration to be 2½ per cent on the amount realized and an equal percentage on the amount distributed in dividend, together with necessary out of pocket expenses. Messrs. J. & O. Messer (of Reading), G. Dugey, upholsterer (Andover), and S. Moreton, plumber (Andover), were selected as the committee of inspection.

WILLIAM CROOK.—On Tuesday the first meeting of the creditors of William Crook, builder, of Castle Street, Salisbury, was held at the office of the Official Receiver, Salisbury. The debtor's summary statement of affairs read thus: —Liabilities to creditors unsecured, £820 13s. 10d.; estimated surplus, £645 14s. 10d. Assets—by book debts, good, doubtful and bad, £37 3s. 9d., estimated to produce £1 15s.; stock-in-trade, £104 11s.; furniture, £40; surplus from property held by creditors, viz., estimated value of securities, £5,800; less due to creditors £4,615 1cs.; estimated surplus, £1,184 10s.—£1,480 16s. 9d.; less preferential creditors for rent, rates, taxes, wages, &c., £14 7s. 4d.; total, £1,460 8s. 8d. There was no deficiency. The debtor attributes his failure solely to depression in trade and the present depreciation of the value of his various building securities. In his observations appended to the statement the Official Receiver said—"I am at a loss to understand how the debtor can state that there is a surplus for creditors. My own belief is that when the estate is worked out the result will show a considerable deficiency." Mr. Fulton
