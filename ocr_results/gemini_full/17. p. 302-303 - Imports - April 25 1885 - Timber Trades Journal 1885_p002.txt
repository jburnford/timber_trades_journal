REVIEWS.
Wood Carving. (Wyman & Sons, Great Queen Street, London, W.C.)
This work, compiled by Mr. Aumonier and edited by Mr. Fred Miller, is carefully and well done, and should be of the greatest practical value to all students popularize which the art of wood-carving, to extend and popularize which the Carpenters' Company and the school at South Kensington are making praiseworthy efforts, and we hope that they will meet with every success. It is an art that has fallen into neglect, but whilst we have such clever craftsmen as Mr. Rogers and Mr. Aumonier we must not despair of its extinction; besides, there can be no question of the superiority of wood ornament over stucco and carton-pierre. Mr. Miller treats the subject practically, theoretically, and historically; there are notes on design as applied to carved wood, and the text is interspersed with 65 illustrations. Altogether the book may be considered an able and timely contribution, and for this reason should command a ready sale.
The Engineers', Millwrights', and Machinists' Practical Assistant. (Crosby Lockwood & Co., 7, Stationers' Hall Court, Ludgate Hill. E.C.)
This is a seventh edition of Mr. W. Templeton's useful manual, carefully revised, with additions, and should be in the pocket of all mechanics in the trades mentioned, for immediate reference, for which purpose, from its size, it is admirably adapted. At the end of the book there are several pages of blank ruled paper for notes.
The Harbours, Docks, and Piers' Gazette. (Vacher & Sons, Parliament Street, Westminster.)
This is a new monthly paper, published at 6d., "for the information of the harbour and dock authorities of the United Kingdom." We think that the range of its usefulness is much wider than this statement implies. The current issue contains large plans of the Royal and Albert and London and St. Katharine Docks, besides a variety of useful news. We welcome this new venture, and wish it every success.
Calvert's Catalogue of Books on Practical Science (99, Great Jackson Street, Hulme, Manchester) contains selections from the catalogues of all publishers, and in this respect it is exhaustive of the more important and necessary works required by those engaged in architecture and building, bridge and shipbuilding, education, civil engineering, decorating, gardening and agriculture, iron and steel manufacture, marine engineering, mechanical engineering, mining and founding, navigation, railway engineering, sanitary engineering, textile manufactures, &c.
