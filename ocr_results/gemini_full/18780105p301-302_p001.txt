[JANUARY 5, 1878.
THE TIMBER TRADES JOURNAL.

LEITH.
(From our own Correspondent.)
January 3rd, 1878.
I have nothing new to communicate for your present issue. We have had very few arrivals this fortnight, and there are no sales to report on. Very little work will be done here this week, nearly all the large works being closed until Monday.
The first sale this year is announced by Messrs. Mitchell, Somervillle, and Co., on the 15th, at the Queen's Dock, consisting of deals and battens as per advertisement.
Messrs. Mitchell, Thomson, and Co., at their saw-mills, Granton, on the 22nd, American yellow pine logs, deals, &c.
Messrs. William Stewart and Co., at the Burgh saw-mills, on the 29th and 30th, various wood goods.
