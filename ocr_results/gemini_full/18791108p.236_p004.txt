Shipping Intelligence.

FREIGHTS.
QUEBEC.—(From the Quebec Chronicle, Oct. 16th).—According to our anticipations freights have taken a strong upward tendency, and this we may say again is partly owing to the scarcity of open tonnage as well as to the apparent improvement in the timber market in England. Grain freights in Montreal seem also to maintain previous quotations, and that fact helps to sustain rates from this port. We can quote transactions at:—Liverpool, 28s. 6d. hardwood; Plymouth, 27s. 6d. timber, 30s. sleepers and deals; London, 75s. day deals. And we learn that 30s. is now being demanded for good cargoes for Liverpool, and as there is an unusual scarcity of tonnage, we think that even higher rates could be obtained.
PENSACOLA.—Messrs. Hyers Bros. under date Sept. 30th, report that tonnage continues in fair demand, and quotations for U. K. and continent are 37s. 6d. and £5 15s. for medium-sized vessels. The clearances for Great Britain are as follows:—Liverpool, Dorothy, 761 tons, 42,846 sawn, 22,000 lumber; Bristol, Arion, 465 tons, 24,556 sawn, 8,000 lumber; Swansea, G. P. Payzant 624 tons, 18,049 hewn, 13,873 sawn, 42,000 lumber; Barrow, City of Ottawa, 912 tons, 10,149 hewn, 38,821 sawn, 31,000 lumber; Greenock, Est, 983 tons, 52,435 sawn, 21,000 lumber; Greenock, Princess Alexandra, 1,342 tons, 63,611 sawn, 41,000 lumber; Grangemouth, Banner, 1,195 tons, 639 hewn, 65,791 sawn, 39,000 lumber.

SEVERE GALE IN THE GULF OF BOTHNIA.—On the 13th and 14th October a heavy gale prevailed in the Gulf of Bothnia, which caused many casualties on the coast of Finland. The Wasa (s), arrived at Hernosund on the 18th inst., reports no fewer than nine stranded vessels at Ronskar, as well as several dead bodies floating on the water.
