ABRIDGMENTS OF BRITISH PATENTS.
635. IMPROVEMENTS IN MACHINERY FOR CUTTING MORTISES. J. Phillips, Kennington, London. Dated February 17th, 1879.

The object of this invention is to construct in a simple and economical manner machinery for cutting mortises in which the tools required for this operation are so arranged that they can be worked by one and the same lever, and may be thrown in or out of working position, and the chisel reversed without removal from its holder. By thus avoiding the dismounting of the tools, a considerable economy in time is effected in the operation. In order to produce a perfect mortise in hardwood, three separate tools are usually required, namely, a boring tool or auger to drill the hole, a chisel to cut the long mortise by means of a series of blows while the work is moved intermittently along under the tool, and a core-driver for clearing out the debris there-from. In order to properly cut the mortise and complete the ends thereof, the position of the cutting chisel, after roughly forming the mortise, must be reversed in its socket, to effect which it is usual for the chisel to be dismounted, reversed in position, and then refixed in the socket, which operations monopolize time. According to this invention these working tools are either arranged radially round a disc, or hollow socket which is capable of being rotated on its axis for the purpose of bringing the working tools in succession, as they may be required, into the position for use. Or the tools may be adapted to a flat plate plunger, so as to be worked up and down there-with, in which case the tools are mounted side by side, so that they may be turned up or down, or drawn up or down, as may be required; the boring tool being in all cases provided with toothed gear, whereby it may be rotated when required to bore holes.
