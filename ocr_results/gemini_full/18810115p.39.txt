MAHOGANY SALE.—At the London Commercial Sale-rooms, on the 13th inst., Messrs. T. Edwards & Son offered for sale the cargo ex Plimsoll, from Minatitlan, of 282 logs of mahogany, containing 109,031 ft., realized 5d. to 6d., average 5 1/2d., and 30 logs of cedar, containing 9,886 ft., realized 4d. to 6d., average 4 7/8d. per ft.
MIDDLESBROUGH.—The Middlesbrough Chamber of Commerce report says that very few cargoes were received during the month, the shipping season being supposed to be closed, but the open weather allowed of the imports being larger than is usually the case in December. The demand was fairly satisfactory, and prices remained about stationary. A good many inquiries were in the market for delivery over 1881. The trade would be considerably extended if the water for storing timber were more adequate at this port ; as it is the trade is somewhat crippled, and is fully as large as it can be under present circumstances. The building industry is becoming brisker with the improving appearance of the staple trade of the district.
