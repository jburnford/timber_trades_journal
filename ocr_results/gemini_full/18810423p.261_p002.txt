APRIL 23, 1881.
SHIPPING INTELLIGENCE.
ST. JOHN, N.B.—Our correspondent writes:—I mentioned last week that the rivers and streams were opening much earlier than usual. Immediately succeeding that, we had several days of ice, and the weather in consequence was unusually severe, the water in the river falling in consequence. The weather has now turned milder, and the prospect is that the river will be free from ice fully three weeks earlier than usual. This will have the effect of enabling the mills at work at the stock early in the season, and as well shippers, as we can now deal with a fair quantity of legal deals. Freights on the whole are easy to reckon on with any degree of certainty, as a great deal depends on the number of new ships that may be brought out during the next two months. BRITISH TIMBER COMPANY.—The fourth miscellaneous concert inaugurated for the benefit of the Bootle Hospital by the employés of the British Timber Company was successfully conducted by themselves and a number of friends recently in the Town Hall, Merton Road, Bootle. The concert was under the patronage of his Worship the Mayor and Mayoress, and other influential residents, and the attractions promised brought together a crowded audience. The secretary, Mr. J. Clayton, may be congratulated upon the successful issue of the venture, which benefits the hospital to the extent of about £12.
