ABERDEEN.
Considerable inconvenience has been experienced by a number of merchants, caused by the late arrivals of f.o.w. shipments from Swedish ports—delayed somewhat unaccountable, as July shipments have in some cases arrived first.
Trade still seems a little languishing, although on the whole recent sales have been brisker; we observe, however, that prices for American timber are not so much ruled by the state of trade as by the quality of the goods.
Messrs. Rust & Son, on Saturday last, cleared out their cargo of waney pine at good prices, the average being 2s. 3d. per cubic ft. Mr. Burgess, of Dyce Saw-mills, had also a good sale, home wood commanding exceptionally high prices, ¼ boards reaching as high as 16s. 6d. per 100 sup. ft.
