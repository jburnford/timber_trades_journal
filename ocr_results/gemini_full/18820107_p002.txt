SHIPBUILDING.—Messrs. Harland & Wolff, of Belfast, have received contracts from the White Star Company for the construction of vessels which will aggregate 60,000 tons, and will cost nearly £2,500,000. Among the vessels contracted for is a sailing ship which, when complete, will be the largest sailing vessel in the world.
REGULATIONS IN THE CANADIAN TIMBER TRADE.—A recent Order in Council has been issued by the Dominion Government which is of interest to settlers in the Canadian West. Under its previsions, a homesteader having no timber on his own farm may obtain permission to cut on available vacant Government lands the following quantities without payment of the ordinary dues—1,800 lineal feet of house timber, logs to be over 12 in. at the small end; 400 roof rails, 30 cords dry wood, 2,000 fence rails. Permits for further quantities can be obtained on payment of the dues that have been fixed.
