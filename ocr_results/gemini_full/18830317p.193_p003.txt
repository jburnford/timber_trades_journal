THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for the full amount.]
J. & F. IVES.—A meeting of the creditors of John Ives and Frederick Ives, builders and contractors, of Shipley, trading as John Ives & Son, was held last week at Bradford. The statement of the debtors' affairs showed liabilities £17,346, and assets £7,948 4s. It was resolved to liquidate by arrangement, and Mr. J. E. Wright, accountant, was appointed the trustee, with a committee of inspection.
KIRKLAND J. & SON.—A meeting of the creditors of Messrs. John Kirkland & Son, Victoria Dock Saw Mills, Dundee, was held on the 9th inst. Mr. Moore, C.A., Edinburgh, trustee, was present, and reported that as yet no offer had been received for the works as a going concern. The meeting agreed to continue the works in the meantime, under the superintendence of Mr. W. R. Calder. The stock of timber is announced for sale by auction on the 27th inst.

JOHN APSEY.—A first meeting was held in this case last week before Mr. Registrar Murray, the bankrupt being described as of 50, Addison Road, N., builder and decorator. Adjudication was made on February 20th, upon the petition of Mr. A. B. A. Etheridge, of Uxbridge Road, as surviving executor of E. Langford, deceased. The accounts were not filed, but several proofs of debt were admitted, and Mr. H. A. Dubois, accountant, of Sergeant's Inn, Chancery Lane, was appointed trustee, with a committee of inspection, namely, Mr. H. G. Sweetlove, 7, South Square, Gray's Inn, and Mr. Edwin Palmer, 28, Golden Square. Next meeting, April 7th.
KELLER BROTHERS.—The debtors, described as of 83, Caledonian Road, new timber merchants and building material dealers, have filed a petition for liquidation, and a sitting was held last week before Mr. Registrar Pepys, for the purpose of registering the resolutions passed by the creditors. The joint accounts disclosed unsecured debts to the amount of £3,799 8s. 2d., debts fully secured £3,541 15s., debts partly secured £3,350 (securities valued at £1,941 14s.), and liabilities on bills discounted expected to rank £32 7s. 6d. Total amount unsecured, £5,240 1s. 8d. Assets £1,192 2s. 9d., of which £1,012 16s. 3d., consisted of stock. It was resolved that the estate should be liquidated by arrangement, and Mr. Earnest Foreman, accountant, 57, Gracechurch Street, was appointed trustee, together with the following committee of inspection: Mr. W. J. Raffety, of Old Broad Street; Mr. J. B. Lee, 80, King William Street; Mr. Alexander Scrutton, of West India Dock Road; Mr. T. R. Groves, 54½, Bishopsgate Street, and Mr. Edward Cattermole, of Albion Road, Islington. In the separate estate of A. F. Speller, whose liabilities were £472 4s. 4d., a compensation of 1s. in the pound was accepted, payable within 28 days from registration. Upon the application of Messrs. Andrews, Wood, & Glazier, and in the absence of any substantial objection, his Honour ordered the resolutions to be registered.
