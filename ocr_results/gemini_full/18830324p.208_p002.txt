THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[IT must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for the full amount.]
WM. PUGSLEY.—The debtor, a builder, at Shepherd's Bush, had filed a petition for liquidation, returning his unsecured debts at £910 6s. 9d., and assets £175. At the first meeting a composition of 2s. in the pound was accepted, payable in four months, and Mr. John Langton, accountant, 68, Cheapside, was appointed trustee. The resolutions were confirmed on the 16th inst., and upon the application of Messrs. Smith & Eldridge, the same have been duly registered.
R. R. PEALER.—A first meeting was held in this case on Wednesday, before Mr. Registrar Pepys, the bankrupt being described as of Gracechurch Buildings, contractor for public works. No accounts were filed, and in the absence of a quorum of creditors a trustee could not be appointed, the meeting being adjourned to Wednesday, April 4th.
