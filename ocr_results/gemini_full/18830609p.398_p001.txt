LEITH.
The imports continue light, a cargo of Holmestrand battens for William Malcolm, and a shipment of pitch pine from Darien for Messrs. Mitchell, Somerville, & Co., together with a few odd consignments of lathwood, &c., making up the total.
On Tuesday Messrs. Mitchell, Somerville, & Co. held a sale by public auction at the old Upper Dock, the timber offered being chiefly old stock. The sale opened with a few parcels of cut sawn pitch pine, which was sold, sawing included. Only a few lots were exposed, and fetched from 1s. per cubic foot. Three or four lots of yellow pine, sawn to boards, followed, and were sold like the pitch pine, sawing included, at from 17½d. to 18d. per cubic foot. All these parcels were merely job lots, having been thrown aside owing to some flaws, making them unfit for the purpose originally intended, or perhaps through their having been sawn to wrong sizes. A good few lots of 7x2, 7x2½, and 8x2 white followed, prices ruling low, doubtless owing to the goods being so very much discoloured. A few lots of 11x3 crown white Petersburg deals were sold at 3½d. per lineal foot, while red Kotka were sold at 1½d. The following is a note of the principal prices obtained :—
