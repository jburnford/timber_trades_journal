R. A. LAMPRELL.—The debtor, trading as a builder and shopfitter at 51, Russell Street, and 80A, Hackford Street, Brixton, has presented a petition for liquidation, and his affairs were brought before Mr. Registrar Brougham on Thursday. Mr. E. D. F. Rymer applied for the appointment of Mr. J. F. French, accountant, Basinghall Street, as receiver of the estate, and for an injunction restraining further proceedings at the suit of Mr. E. T. Gething, 55, Holland Street, Brixton, for the recovery of £36 5s. 4d., and Mr. R. Morris, of 239, Kennington Road, for £10 11s. The liabilities were returned at £1,087, and the assets, consisting of stock-in-trade and book debts, were valued at £450. His Honour made the appointment and granted an injunction.

W. HAMPTON.—A second general meeting of the creditors of William Hampton, builder and contractor, of the Steam Saw and Joinery Works, Hoddesdon, Herts, is announced to be held at the office of Mr. George Rutter Fletcher, 14, Finsbury Square, City, on Monday next, the 9th of July, and a majority in number and three-fourths in value of the creditors then assembled may confirm the resolution come to at the first general meeting, or a majority in number representing three-fourths in value of such creditors may, by resolution, declare that the affairs of the above-named person may be liquidated by arrangement and not in bankruptcy.
CHARLES BAILEY.—An adjourned meeting for public examination was held in this case on Saturday before Mr. Registrar Murray, the bankrupt being described as of Alma Terrace, Fulham Road, builder. His unsecured debts were stated in the accounts at £2910 2s. 8d., and debts fully secured £1,398 10s.; assets £415 10s. Mr. Savidge appeared for the trustee and did not oppose. Mr. F. C. Willis supported. The bankrupt was allowed to pass his examination.
S. CRISP.—The debtor, Samuel Crisp, of 249, Commercial Road, Whitechapel, and 10, Adding[?] Road, Bow, builder and contractor, had filed a petition for liquidation; and Mr. Davies (Gresham & Davies) applied on Tuesday for registration of the resolutions purporting to have been come to by the creditors. The unsecured debts were stated in the accounts at £1,398 16s., and secured debts £991; assets £306 18s. It was resolved that the estate should be liquidated by arrangement, and Mr. A. W. Blunt, accountant, 14, Queen Victoria Street, was appointed trustee. Mr. S. Woolf appeared for opposing creditors. In the result it was found that the resolutions had not been passed by the statutory majority of creditors, and registration was accordingly refused.
G. COLLINGWOOD.—An adjourned meeting for public examination was held before Mr. Registrar Murray on Saturday in the bankruptcy of George Collingwood, now or late of Chatsworth Road, Clapton Park, timber merchant and builder. The unsecured debts were stated in the accounts at £1,961 7s. 10d.; and debts partly secured £2,900; assets £2,400 (the security being valued at £3,464), and preferential claims £50; total unsecured £3,075 7s. 10d. Assets £142 6s. The trustee (Mr. Foreman) said that he had examined the bankrupt and had informed him that the accounts were not exactly what he required. The bankrupt offered to give further information, and did not object to another adjournment. Adjourned to the 14th inst.
