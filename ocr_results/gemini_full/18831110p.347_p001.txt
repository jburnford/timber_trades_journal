NOVEMBER 10, 1883.]

BUILDING NEWS.
It is proposed to build new baths at Exeter, at a cost of £3,000.
At Pudsey (Yorks) new Board schools are to be erected at Greenside.
At Southport a new church is to be built for the parish of St. Philip's.
At Bourn (Lincoln) a new Board school and master's house are to be built.
The Proest rural sanitary authority propose to build a hospital for infectious diseases.
At Eastbourne (Sussex) a new Town Hall and other municipal buildings are to be built.
The Liverpool Town Council have decided to extend the Walker Art Gallery, at a cost of £5,500.
Various amounts have been promised towards a new church and school to be erected at Doncaster.
Mr. Inglis, C.E., of Plymouth, has prepared a design of a pier which it is proposed to construct at Newlyn.
The Marquis of Hartington laid the foundation stone of the new church at Buxton last week.
Mr. E. Atkinson has the contract for the new public buildings which are to be erected at Middlesbrough.
Manufactories and offices are to be built at Marsh Hill, Homerton, for Dr. Gilder, at a cost of £3,000.
The tender of Messrs. Spencer & Co., of Ryder Street, has been accepted for rebuilding Oscar House, Lewisham.
The Brighton Town Council have unanimously vetoed the schemes for introducing tramways into the town.
New gasworks of an extensive character, costing £150,000, have just been completed and opened at Bradford.
The foundation stone of a new Free Church was laid last week at Leadhills, near Glasgow, the cost of which will be about 2,000.
The building of a new viaduct at Holbrook, near Kidderminster, has been commenced by the Great Western Railway Company.
The attractions of Eastbourne are to be increased by the erection of a large covered floral hall on a site near Terminus Road.
It has been resolved to erect a new chapel for the Baptist denomination, near the Alexandra Dock district, Newport, at a cost of £1,200.
The memorial stones of a new church at Farnworth were laid recently.
There will be accommodation for about 400, and the cost will be £2,470.
The foundation stone of a new town hall and public buildings has been laid at Middlesbrough.
The buildings are estimated to cost nearly £100,000.
The Mayor of Newark has laid the foundation stone of a new covered market in that town.
The cost of erecting the same will amount to over £2,000.
At the Greenock Dean of Guild Court last week it was stated that the estimated value of new buildings, alterations, and additions was about £37,000 during last year.
The preliminaries for the construction of a dock and for the deepening of the Ribble are being rapidly pushed on.
The estimated cost of the works is about £500,000 sterling.
Lord Derby opened the new wing of the Convalescent Hospital at Southport last week, erected by the trustees of the Lancashire Cotton Famine Fund at a cost of £27,000.
Mr. W. S. Gilbert, the dramatic author, has just built for himself a dwelling-house in Harrington Gardens, South Kensington, on which it is said he has expended £30,000 on the house alone, before an article of furniture is put into it.

THE TIMBER TRADES JOURNAL.

TENDERS OPEN.
Alterations and Additions.—The Chevington School Board invite tenders for alterations to be made to Chevington North Schools, Broomhill.
Plans may be seen at office of Mr. William Webb, Newgate Street, Morpeth, to whom tenders are to be sent on the 17th November.
Chapel.—Persons desirous of tendering for Gosforth Wesleyan Chapel may see drawings on application at office of Mr. J. J. Lish, Scottish Chambers, Grainger Street West, Newcastle-on-Tyne.
Tenders to be delivered to Mr. D. A. Holdsworth, Gosforth Terrace, Gosforth, by 12th November.
Chapel, &c.—Persons willing to tender for erection of a new Congregational Chapel, and taking down and re-erecting as Sunday school and classrooms the present chapel and school at Dresden, near Longton, may see the drawings at offices of Messrs. W. Sugden & Son, Leek.
Tenders to be sent to Rev. J. H. Howshall, Dresden, on 24th inst.
Chapels, &c.—Tenders are invited by Edmonton Local Board for buildings in connection with new cemetery in the district of Edmonton, comprised in two chapels, loop, and mortuary, fencings, &c.
Drawings may be seen at office of Mr. F. J. Ricketts, Church Street, Edmonton.
Tenders to Mr. Charles S. Houlder, at offices of Local Board, Church Street, Edmonton, by 13th inst.
Coastguard Station.—Tenders for the erection of a new coastguard station at Wells, in county of Norfolk, will be received at the Director of Works Department, Admiralty, 71, Spring Gardens, London, S.W., at or before noon on November 9th.
The drawings and specifications may be seen at the office, or upon application to the Coastguard Station, at Wells.
Bills of quantities may be obtained on application at the office.
Dwellings.—The Commissioners of Sewers of City of London will meet in Guildhall, on 20th November, to receive tenders for erection of five blocks of artisans' and labourers' dwellings at Petticoat Square, Middlesex Street, in accordance with plans, to be seen at the office of the engineer.
Tenders to Mr. Henry Blake, Sewers Office, Guildhall, delivered on said day.
Hospital.—Persons desirous of tendering for erection of Burnley and District Hospital are requested to communicate with Messrs. Wm. Waddington & Son, 5, Grimshaw Street, Burnley, and 25, Cross Street, Manchester, before the 15th inst.
Hotel, &c.—Persons desirous of tendering for work required in erection of hotel, refreshment-room, and shops, in Mill Street, Hanley, for Charles Butters, Esq., may see plans at offices of Messrs. R. Scrivener & Sons, Howard Place, Hanley, on the 12th inst.
Houses.—Tenders are required for erection of twelve small houses at Parkeston, near Harwich.
Drawings can be seen at offices of Messrs. Whitmore & Reeves, 14, Devonshire Square, Bishopsgate, and at Chelmsford.
Municipal Buildings.—Tenders are invited for whole of works required in alterations and additions to proposed municipal buildings at Heywood.
Tenders to be sent to Mr. Alfred Wallis, Heywood, not later than the 13th November, instead of the 30th October as previously advertised.
Post Office, &c.—Persons desirous of tendering for works connected with new post office and residence at Fleetwood can see plans on application at office of Mr. C. Pearson Shaw, 37, St. Peter's Place, Fleetwood, till the 24th, upon which day tenders must be delivered to postmaster.
Residence.—Persons desirous of tendering for whole of the work required in building a residence, stabling for fifteen horses, carriage-houses, barn and loose boxes, &c., in Windermere Park, can see plans at office of the architect.
Tenders to be sent by the 16th November to Mr. Eli Cox, Government Offices, Highgate, Kendal.
Schools.—The County Justices of Durham invite tenders for erection of schools at Earl's House Farm.
Plans may be inspected at office of county engineer, the Shire Hall, Durham.
Tenders to county engineer by November 17th.
Vicarage.—Persons wishing to tender for building a new vicarage at Penkhull are requested to apply to Messrs. Lynam & Rickman, Stoke-on-Trent.

Best goods only.
