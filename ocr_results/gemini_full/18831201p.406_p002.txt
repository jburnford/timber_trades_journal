Chicago is the greatest lumber market in the world says the *Lumberman*. The single item of sawed lumber received there in 1881 would lay an inch flooring 14 ft. wide round the earth at the equator. The amount of lumber manufactured in the three states of Michigan, Wisconsin, and Minnesota during 1881 would lay such a floor 50 ft. wide.
The remains of the Charlemagne bridge over the Rhine at Mayence are now being excavated, and the timber is found to be as black as ebony and very rich. Mr. Joseph Patrick, of Western Sandstrasse 1, Frankfort-on-the-Main, has had some carved chairs, with pressed leather backs and seats, manufactured from some of the best piles.
