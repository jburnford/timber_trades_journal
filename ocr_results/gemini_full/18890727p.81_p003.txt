BUILDING NEWS.
During the past forty years the London Corporation has expended £318,981 on Billingsgate Market, £504,842 on the Metropolitan Cattle Market at Islington, £962,000 on the London Central Market, £332,000 on the Poultry Market, £465,500 on the Central Fruit Market (lately a fish market), £247,800 upon Leadenhall Market, and £379,500 upon the Deptford Market — altogether, an expenditure of £3,210,623 for market accommodation, and exclusive of approaches.
A new public library is to be built at Paddington at a cost of £2,500.
The London County Council have sanctioned a loan of £3,000 for defraying the cost of the erection of a public library and museum at Rotherhithe.
The pier at Southport is to be rebuilt, and a new pavilion, seating 500 persons, is also to be erected.
Sir Edward Cecil Guinness has notified to the trustees of the People's Palace his intention of increasing his original donation of £9,000 for the erection of a winter garden to a sum of £14,000, in order that the people of East London may be provided with a perfect winter garden of the best possible design.
A new station is to be built at Heckmondwike.
The Carlisle Corporation have made an application for a loan of about £7,000 for local improvement purposes.
