BUILDING NEWS.
The Cleator Moor reservoir is to be restored.
A new public hall is to be erected at Lilliesleaf, N.B.
A circus is to be constructed in East Ingram Street, Glasgow.
Additions, costing £987, are to be made to the Homerton Workhouse.
A pavilion is to be built at Longton Park. It is estimated to cost £4,000.
The Lancashire Bridge over the River Mersey at Stockport is to be widened and otherwise improved.
The Local Government Board have sanctioned a loan of £10,000 for the erection of public offices, New Swindon.
On Monday last a handsome new county hall was opened at Salisbury by the Mayor. Mr. F. Griffin, timber merchant, responded at the inaugural dinner on behalf of the corporation.
A building which was being converted into a spinning mill fell in at Dundee on Tuesday, seriously injuring seven workmen, who were removed to the infirmary. The workmen had piled the slates from the roof on the top floor, which gave way under the excessive weight, carrying the other floors with it.
