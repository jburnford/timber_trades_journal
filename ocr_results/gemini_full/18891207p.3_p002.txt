It is announced that the revenue of the Greenock Harbour Trust for the three months of the current financial year shows an increase of 29 per cent. over the receipts for the corresponding period of 1888.

The imports of Irish timber to the west coast have been considerable for some time, and embrace all varieties of fir and hardwoods. There have been a good many cargoes of late, and it seems to find a ready market, most of it being transhipped on rail for immediate consumption.
