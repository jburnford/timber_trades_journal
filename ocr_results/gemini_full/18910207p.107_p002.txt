SWEDISH NOTES
(From the Svensk Travaru Tidning.)
In its market report, the Svensk Travaru Tidning says that at no time in the history of the timber trade has the market been so utterly demoralised and shippers to hold aloof. controvertible facts to they possibly can be, towards the summer months.
It has been doubted whether the reduced cutting would affect prices already this summer, as the stock for f.o.w. is said to be very large. But this is only an attempt by agents to influence prices in favour of buyers. The Saw-mill and Timber Export Society, in its report for 1890 (shortly to be published), has ascertained that within the Gefle-Haparanda district the stock for shipment is about 39,000 standards less than last year.
An important firm of agents in London telegraphs to the paper that there is a panic in the London timber trade, and the paper emphatically advises shippers to keep quiet till next month, and the paper does the same.
By a mistake, it was stated in the last number that the credit granted by Finnish exporters is three months; it is four months, and Gothenburg shipments are also made on four months' credit. How is it that the Timber Trades Journal cannot see the advisability of reducing the six months' credit to three months? We have to pay cash for what we buy from England.
Our London correspondent gives a very pessimist view of the situation in London. He could, if he liked, give the names of four or five more firms which will have to stop, as sure as night follows day; only one of these imports Scandinavian goods, and not very much. He ascribes the late failures principally to the six months' credit. This system must cease, being a curse to the trade.
