FAILURES AND INSOLVENCIES.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for the whole or part of the full amount.]

BETTS WILLIAM,
of Distillery Street and Troy Street, Hull, fruiterer, and now builder, appeared for his public examination.
The liabilities were £257 17s. 11d.; deficiency, £257 17s. 11d.
Mr. O. Sadd appeared for the debtor.
The case was heard at the Norwich Bankruptcy Court.
The debtor said he commenced business as a speculative builder in Distillery Street two years ago.—In reply to further questions, he said he was not a sporting man, but he had carried a borrowed gun for a friend, and was betting for the last two years.
The bet was never more than 10s.
After being examined the debtor was allowed to pass.
The case was closed.
A list of creditors was published in our Journal on January 31.

BLAZEY, BENJAMIN,
bricklayer and builder, St. Austell.—Liabilities, £209 18s. 9½d.; deficiency, £198 18s. 9½d.—In reply to the Official Receiver, the debtor said that when he started business he had little capital.
He only kept a pocket ledger, and no record of his cash takings never went through his banking account.
His trade had fallen off, and for the last two years he had known that his income was not sufficient to meet his liabilities.
He had a loss over a contract which he did not expect to recover.
He went on trading.
