INTERESTING FOSSIL.—A few days ago the navvies working at the new railway on the side of the Crieff and Comrie Railway, a little side of the town, came upon a large block of stone lying about 8 ft. below the surface of the ground. On examination the stone was found to have been a root-cutting implement, about 2 foot in length, having a diameter at the thick end of 7 in. and at the thin end of 3 in. The lower end appears as if it had been sharpened by some such instrument, and has a distinct appearance of being cut with a tool, and is quite smooth. The various marks of the tool are distinctly visible at the thick end, and in an experienced wood merchant's opinion it is the cutting of a labourer, but much heavier than a stone axe.
