SHIPPING DISASTERS.
The first loss of any consequence put on the books this week was the steamship Ardandhu, reported ashore at Hough Tiree, and likely to become a total wreck, while on a voyage from Riga to Fleetwood with a cargo of railway sleepers. The St. Olaf (s), of Drontheim, after having loaded wood in Hommelvik, broke her shaft, and was towed into Drontheim, September 25th, for repairs.
The schooner Niord, from Archangel, after being thirty-two days out, put into Ramsey, September 29th, through stress of weather. She was laden with timber for Liverpool. Owing to the heavy gale night of September 28th, the deck cargo had to be thrown overboard when near the Calf of Man.
The barque Kate Burrill, of Yarmouth, N.S., arrived at Ringsend (Dublin) on Wednesday, with loss of part of deck cargo, timber; is strained, and making water.
The telegram from Queenstown, September 30th, stated:-Manx Queen arrived here, jettisoned a few logs of mahogany from deck cargo.
The British steamer Springbox, from Danzig, for Wisbeach, with sleepers, put into Copenhagen on Wednesday, after being ashore at Anholt. She was examined by divers, and found to have received no damage.
The Dutch brig Regalia, laden with wood, stranded on Wednesday night at Vinga, but got off afloat without assistance, although full of water.
