Black and White recently contained the second portion of an article on the building of the Ophir at Messrs. Napier's yard. It is excellently illustrated, and we learn that, in good times, the firm employ nearly 3,000 hands. They employ several blind men in screwing bolts and spinning oakum.
FIRE IN GLASGOW.—A fire recently broke out in the premises of Messrs. Thomas Kay & Co., packing-box makers, 21, Cathedral Street, Glasgow, caused, it is supposed, by an overheated stove igniting the woodwork. The firemen experienced great difficulty in overcoming the flames, owing to the dense smoke and to the great amount of gas that escaped from a broken pipe. The building was at one time used as a church, and is owned by the North British Railway Company.
