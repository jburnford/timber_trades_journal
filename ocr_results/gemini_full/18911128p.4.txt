BUILDING
ARBROATH.—The directors of the Caledonian railway station have resolved to proceed with an additional platform.
PERTH SEWERAGE.—At a special meeting of the interception drainage district of the County Council, the Committee are to proceed with the works.
CONTRACTS OPEN.
ABERDEEN.—For the works of additions and alterations at Tonley House, Tough. Plans and schedules of quantities to be seen with Messrs. Matthews and Mackenzie, architects, Aberdeen, and at the house of Tonley on 30th inst. Tenders to be lodged with the architects on Dec. 2.
EDINBURGH.—For the ironfounder, plumber, and slater work, ironmongers' work, and bellhangers' work in connection with additions and alterations to buildings, for H.M. Commissioners of Works, for three years from Jan. 1, 1892. Commissioners' forms of tender, with schedules, with conditions of contract, may be obtained at H.M. Office of Works, Parliament Square, Edinburgh, between 11 a.m. and 3 p.m. Tenders for the mason and joiner work to be sent to the First Commissioner of H.M. Works, &c., 12, Whitehall Place, London, S.W., on Dec. 1.
