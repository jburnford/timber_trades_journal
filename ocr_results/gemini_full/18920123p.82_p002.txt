DISASTER.
Advices from Varde, January 17th, state :-At the end of last month and beginning of this a number of deals have washed ashore with the following marks:-"H. E. C.," "Three Triangles," "G. W. L.," "T. S.," and "X.," also a good many without marks and part of a name board with name "George Walker."

Seventeenth Annual Special Issue of the
Timber Trades Journal
AND SAW-MILL ADVERTISER
WILL BE PUBLISHED ON
SATURDAY, FEBRUARY 13, 1892,
And will consist of about 250 Pages, bound in an Ornamental Wrapper.
COPIES OF THIS ISSUE WILL BE SENT DIRECT BY POST TO
10000 CONSUMERS OF WOOD
In all parts of the Kingdom, who do not regularly read the Journal, from a carefully compiled list of all the principal Firms engaged
in the various Industries in which Wood is largely used, and who are consequently the Timber Merchants' best customers.

AMONGST THE UNIQUE FEATURES OF THIS SPECIAL ISSUE WILL BE
(1) REVIEW of THE TIMBER TRADE of 1891 at all the Ports of England, Scotland, Wales, and Ireland, divided into the following sections, with a Portrait of a well-known representative Timber Merchant in each District:-
London
South Coast
Eastern Counties
Humber Ports
North-East Ports
Liverpool and West Coast
South Coast
Bristol Channel and Western Ports
Firth of Forth
North of Scotland
Glasgow and West of Scotland
Irish Ports
(2) REVIEW OF THE MAHOGANY AND HARD-WOOD Trades at the leading centres.
(3) REVIEW of THE HOME-GROWN TIMBER Trade in England, Scotland, and Ireland.
(4) THE WORLD'S TIMBER SUPPLY-A record of the Export Timber Trade in Sweden, Norway, Finland, Russia, Germany, Austria, Canada, United States, &c.
(5) THE CONTINENTAL AND COLONIAL TIMBER Trade in France, Belgium, Holland, Denmark, Spain, Australia, and the Cape.
The Reviews and the Statistics accompanying them form a complete record of the year's trade not elsewhere obtainable.
