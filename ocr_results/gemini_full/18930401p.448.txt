THE EGAN COMPANY TAKES THE MEDAL-The latest triumph of ingenuity brought out by the Egan Company, of Cincinnati, Ohio, U.S.A., has just been awarded the "medal of superiority" by the American Institute of New York City.
The machine that was given such high honours is the triple drum sander (for the polishing and sandpapering of all kinds of woods) which has of late become so famous for beautiful and reliable work.
THE WORLD'S FAIR-The Irish Village which the Countess of Aberdeen has organised as an exhibit at the World's Fair, Chicago, will be the first object the visitor will encounter on leaving the railway station for the Woman's Building.
The gateway is modelled from that at King Cormac's chapel, Rock of Cashel, and just beyond it will be the turnstiles where visitors must pay for admission.
A fine replica of the cloister from Muckross Abbey, Killarney, has been erected near the gateway, and then come the fifteen cottages forming the village.
Workers in Irish bog-oak will occupy one of the cottages, and Connemara marble and jewellery will be on sale.
Chests of tea and blackthorn sticks are also to be seen.
