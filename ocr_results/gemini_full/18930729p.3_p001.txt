ROYAL SCOTTISH ARBORICULTURAL SOCIETY.
PROGRAMME OF ANNUAL EXCURSION.
Members starting from Edinburgh will leave the Princes Street Station of the Caledonian Railway at 2 p.m. on Monday, 7th August. Special saloon carriages will be provided for the members, and will be at their service throughout the excursion. Travelling via Carlisle and London and North-Western Railway, Willesden Junction, London, will be reached about 10.30 p.m., and thence via Clapham Junction to London and South-Western Railway, the party will arrive at Windsor the same night.
The headquarters of the society during the stay at Windsor will be at the White Hart Hotel. Starting from the White Hart at 8.30 a.m. on Tuesday, 8th August, the party will visit Windsor Castle, the Home Gardens, and the Prince Consort's Shaw Farm. Thereafter they will drive through Windsor Great Park and Forest, when the following among other objects of interest will be seen :—The Flemish Farm, the Prince Consort's Oak, William the Conqueror's Oak, Queen Adelaide's Oak, the Queen's Jubilee Oak, the Prince Consort's workshop, the statue of the Prince Consort, Virginia Water, the Cedar Drive, Belvidere Fort, the Obelisk, the Rhododendron Drive, and a plantation of 200 years' growth, and thence by the Long Walk to Windsor, arriving there at about 7.30 p.m.
The annual dinner will be held at 8 p.m. at the White Hart Hotel.
On Wednesday, the 9th of August, the members will leave Windsor, in carriages, at 10 a.m., to visit the woodlands on the estate of the Duke of Westminster at Cliveden, and of Lady Fortescue at Dropmore, and the famed Burnham Beeches. The party will then return to headquarters, to proceed by special train at 7 p.m., from Windsor to Southampton. The headquarters of the society during the visit to the New Forest will be at the South-Western Railway Hotel, Southampton.
On Thursday, the 10th August, the excursionists will cross Southampton Water by steamer, to Hythe, and thence drive through the southern portion of the New Forest, around Lyndhurst to Mark Ash, returning to Beaulieu, to Lyndhurst, where lunch will be served in the Verderer's Hall in the Queen's House. Afterwards the features of interest around Lyndhurst will be inspected, and the drive continued to Mark Ash, returning to Boldrewood, returning to Southampton at about 7.30 p.m.
On Friday, 11th August, the drive will be resumed to the northern parts of the New Forest, via Lyndhurst and Rufus's Stone, Fritham ; walking through Bentley's Plain, Fritham Sloden Old Wood, and Island Thorn Younger Plantations, driving thence to Stoney Cross, where lunch will be served. Resuming the drive through the forest, Southampton will be reached in time for the 7.25 p.m. train for London. On arrival at Waterloo Station, London, at 9.49 p.m., a 'bus will convey the members to the [?] Hotel, Pimlico.
On Saturday, the 12th August, the members will go by train at 8.45 a.m. from Victoria Station to Kew Gardens Station, and, by special permission of the director, they will visit the Royal Gardens in the forenoon. The party will return from Kew Gardens Station at 12 44 p.m. to Earl's Court Station to visit the Forestry Exhibition, on the special invitation of the directors. This will close the programme of the excursion, and those desiring to return to Scotland the same night can catch the 8.50 p.m. or 10 p.m. train from Euston Station, London, for Edinburgh and the north. By a special agreement with the railway companies, however, any member of the excursion is allowed the privilege to return from Euston Station by any train within a fortnight of the date of issue of the railway ticket.
