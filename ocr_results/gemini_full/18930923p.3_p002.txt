SEPTEMBER 23, 1893.

TECHNICAL EDUCATION.
The following examination questions are among those which were recently set by the City and Guilds of London Institute :—
CARPENTRY.
Ordinary Grade.
1. Give a short historical account of a tree. Describe the method of converting timber, the method of seasoning, and the tree into marketable stuff. State the method of cutting up a fir tree into scantlings.
2. What do you understand by the terms "pine," "fir," and "deal"? State the nature and properties of the best varieties of timber adopted for the proper construction of joinery, and explain with sketches what use any samples shown to you are put to.
3. Describe fully the tools which are adopted for the proper construction of joinery.
4. State for what particular purpose these tools are used :—Chisel, firmer, paring chisel, gouge, jack-plane, smoothing plane, try plane, rebate plane, moulding plane, plough, sash, fillister, and bead.
5. Give sketches of the following joints : —Halved dovetail, common dovetail, lap or half-blind dovetail, mitre or secret dovetail, double mortice, tusk tenon, foxtail wedging. State briefly for what purpose each joint is particularly used.
6. Draw, to one-quarter full size, a section and elevation, showing the construction of the middle rail jointing into the stile of a stile door, which is to be framed with "raised") and bolection moulded panel, and is ovolo moulded on the solid part and prepared for glass. State what edge you would put on the glass, and why.
7. Sketch the following mouldings :— Torus, ogee, Roman ogee, cavetto, scotia, cyma recta, cyma reversa, quirked bead and tongue, lamb's tongue, and state where you would find them in the following cases :—(1) a mitre of straight with curved work, as in the following case, a circular head in a square corner to a bolection moulding ; (2) a panel in a jamb lining, the two ends of which follow the curve of a semicircular panel; (3) a circular head in a square meeting a mitred return.
8. Draw, to a scale of 1½ inch to a foot, a plan of a deal cased frame with double hung sashes, set 4½ inches from the face of wall, clear between the pulley stiles, showing through the junction of the meeting rails and through the oak cill.
9 Draw, to a scale of 1 inch to a foot, a quarter partition, with double doors in the centre 3 ft. wide, for an opening 8 ft. wide and 10 ft. high. Explain the principles observed in framing.
