FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that creditors whose names are not published hold security for their debts for the full amount.]
BOOTH, HENRY, builder and contractor, Baddow Road, Romford.—The above came up for his public examination at the Chelmsford Bankruptcy Court. Mr. Registrar Duffield presided. The accounts showed gross liabilities £735 17s. 5d., and a deficiency of £685 17s. 5d. Among the unsecured creditors were Brown & Co., timber merchants, £100, whose claim was disputed. In answer to questions by the Official Receiver (Mr. Cecil Mercer), the bankrupt said that on December 15th, 1894, he was bankrupt at the Chelmsford [?]. He obtained his discharge on July 10th, 1896, and had commenced business in the preceding January. He was aware that he had contracted any debts between March, 1896. Since his discharge he had be carried on business in his wife's name. He obtained his discharge.
