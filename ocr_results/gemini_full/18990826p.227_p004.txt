FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for their claims, sometimes for the full amount.]
BRADING, THOMAS, builder and contractor, Ventnor.—The debtor's statement of affairs shows gross liabilities amounting to £614 10s. 4d., and after deducting from this the value of securities held by a creditor fully secured, there was a surplus of 15s. 1d. as the sum expected to rank was £350. The available assets were returned at £264 10s. 4d., leaving a deficiency of £85 14s. 11d. The public examination was fixed for the 14th August last at the Town Hall, Newport, Isle of Wight. Bankruptcy Court, Newport and Ryde, adjourned till Monday next.
CLARKE, GEORGE H., builder, of Cranley Gardens, Wallington, and carrying on business at Sutton, under the style of George Clarke and Co.—The statutory meeting of creditors concerned under this failure was held on Thursday day last at the offices of the Official Receiver for the Croydon District, Railway-approach, London Bridge, S.E., under the presidency of Mr. Acheson, Assistant Official Receiver. The statement of affairs filed by the debtor disclosed gross liabilities amounting to £2,158 17s. 4d., of which £2,123 2s. 7d. was expected to rank. To fully secured creditors the value of the securities held by them is carried out as an asset. To rank for dividend £2,158 17s. 4d. The securities in the estimated surplus of £35 14s. 11d. are returned at £757 15s., from which £2,005 has to be deducted for the claims of creditors payable in full, leaving £2,123 2s. 7d.
