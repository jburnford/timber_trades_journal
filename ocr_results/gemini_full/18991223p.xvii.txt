DUNDEE.
Bell & Sime, Ltd., held their annual sale of timber, and their Christmas dinner to their customers, on Monday last. There was a large attendance at both functions. Satisfactory rates were got at the sale, but in view of the present rise in price of wood, consumers purchased for immediate use only. Amongst those present were Provost Edmiston, Rutherglen; Messrs. J. Findlay, H. McTaggart, J. Mabon, Stewart, and J. Laidlaw, Glasgow; J. Harris, St. Andrews; J. Cowan, Grangemouth; C. Cameron, Acharn; S. Carmichael, Coupar; J. Brown, Buckhaven; D. Morrison, Perth; D. Ramsay, Carnoustie; J. Smith, Callander; J. Guthrie, Dunblane, &c., &c., and a number of local gentlemen.

BURNTISLAND.
Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday, 1st, 2nd and 3rd prox., are to be observed as holidays at Burntisland Harbour. Shippers are requested to note this.

GLASGOW.
The Corporation Tramways invite tenders for the extension and alterations to White Vale Depot. The whole work is to be let as one contract, and offers have to be lodged with J D. Marwick, Town Clerk, not later than Tuesday, 2nd January next, at 5 p.m.
The Glasgow and South Western Railway are also prepared to receive tenders for St. Enoch Station extension. The work comprises steel work, mason work, slater work, glazier, and joiner work. Tenders to be lodged not later than Monday, 25th inst., with F H. Gillies, Secretary.

OBAN.
The Callander & Oban Railway ask offers for extension of quay, roadway, &c., at Oban. John Anderson, Secretary, 58, Bath Street, Glasgow, will receive tenders up to Saturday, 20th January, 1900.
