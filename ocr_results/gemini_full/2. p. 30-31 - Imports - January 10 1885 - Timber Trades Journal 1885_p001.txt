ELECTRIC LIGHTING IN STEAMSHIPS.—It was stated at a recent meeting of the Institution of Mechanical Engineers, that although not more than three years have elapsed since the first application of incandescent electric lighting to the general illumination of steamships, the advantages accruing therefrom have been so universally recognised that more than 150 ships have been fitted with it, and scarcely a man-of-war or a first-class passenger steamer now leaves the builder's yard without having it on board.
