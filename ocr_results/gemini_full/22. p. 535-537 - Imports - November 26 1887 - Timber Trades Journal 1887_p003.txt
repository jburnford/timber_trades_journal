THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases
creditors whose names are published in these
lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for
the full amount.]
APPLEBY, JAMES.-At the Hull Bank-
ruptcy Court, on Monday last, Mr. A. M.
Jackson appeared for Mr. James Appleby,
timber merchant, of Hull, and made an appli-
cation for discharge. The Official Receiver
opposed the application for the reason that the
debtor's books had not been properly kept,
and did not show the whole of his transactions;
also that the debtor had admitted that his
deficiency account had been made up from
memory, that he had traded after he knew he
was insolvent, and other allegations. There
was a deficiency of about £4,300. Mr. Jack-
son, in addressing his Honour in support of
the application, stated that the debtor's
education was imperfect, and that he had to
employ a bookkeeper. He lost about £8,000
over the buying of a forest in Finland, besides
losing large sums of money on land and
property in Hull, and other losses. Mr.
Jackson urged that the debtor should not be
punished for having made unfortunate invest-
ments. The debtor was called, and examined.
His Honour was of opinion that he could not
say that the debtor had not been unfortunate,
or that his conduct had been so open to
reflection that he could visit him with punish-
ment on that account. However, he could
not take that view with regard to the books,

and having pointed out the seriousness of the
offence, said the discharge must be suspended
for six months.
BAINTON, HENRY, builder, Leeds.-This
debtor appeared at the Leeds Bankruptcy
Court on Tuesday last for his public examina-
tion. The Official Receiver stated that the
accounts had been amended as required, and he
had no objection to the debtor passing. He
explained that in this case property had been
built by the bankrupt, that the material had
been paid for, and that a conveyance had
been made to a son-in-law, who mortgaged the
property to a building society. The son-in-law
was simply trustee for the bankrupt, and the
property should have been referred to in the
bankrupt's statement as his own. That state-
ment was now being inserted in the balance-
sheet, and he thought that would be sufficient.
The examination, therefore concluded.
BAYES, THOS., builder, Winchester, and
Eastleigh.-The sitting for the public examina-
tion herein was held at the Winchester Bank-
ruptcy Court last week, before Judge Leonard.
The bankrupt was examined at some length
by the Official Receiver (Mr. J. C. Moberley).
He stated that he commenced business with
£100, advanced by his sister, and £60 by his
niece, who were now included in the list of
creditors. His book produced showed balance
credit account £584 10s. 11d. at the begin-
ning of 1887. The payment of £64 3s. 7d. to
Bosworth was to meet bills coming due to
Darton & Co. He did not know who had the
4th for the £70 to Bosworth on the
June last, but it was a three months' bill.
He had received accommodation from Gates,
Darton, & Co. before he went into business,
and now made them advances of money.
When he began business he owed neither £100
nor £50 to Darton & Co. They assisted him and
he assisted them. On the application of the
Official Receiver the examination was
adjourned for a month, and debtor was
directed to prepare further particulars relating
to Gates, Darton, & Co.
CARD, GEORGE HENRY, builder, New
Swindon.-At the Swindon Bankruptcy Court
last week, before his Honour Judge Caillard,
Mr. A. E. Withy applied on behalf of this
bankrupt for his order of discharge. The Offi-
cial Receiver (Mr. H. C. Tombs) stated that a
receiving order was made on the debtor's own
petition. The debtor had complied with the
requirements of the Act and had not committed
any misdemeanour. The unsecured debts
amounted to £164 3s. 3d. and the amount of
assets had been £71 12s. 6d., and a dividend of 6s.
had been paid. The bankrupt's book debts
were fairly got in, but his books were only in-
differently kept. On the latter ground he (the
Official Receiver) asked for a suspension of the
debtor's discharge. Mr. Withy said, with regard
to the indifferent bookkeeping, the debtor
was not well educated, and was incompetent to
keep books accurately. His Honour said the
creditors alone suffered from that cause. Mr.
Withy asked that the suspension should only
be for a short time, so that the debtor might
be able to get a living for his wife and family.
Mr. Tombs said the debtor had acted straight-
forwardly throughout, and under these circum-
stances his Honour ordered the discharge to be
suspended for a month only.
BISHOP & WHITE, builders, Boscombe,
Bournemouth. - The public examination
of George White, of this firm, was held
last week at the Poole Bankruptcy Court,
before Mr. Registrar Dickinson. Bishop
passed his examination at the
last Court. White's examination had been
adjourned to enable him to give particulars as
to "expectations" of money on the death of
his aunt, whose address he would not give. In
questions he admitted that his wife
wrote to the Carlisle Probate Court and
obtained a copy of his uncle's will, and that
he showed a copy of that to a money-lender
named Gascoigne. Both copies had been
"lost." He would not say if the money-lender
mentioned to advance £300 on the
strength of the will, and would have to give
notice to the trustees under it. Debtor also
refused to answer whether or not he took the
copy to Mr. Bazalgette's office. As the debtor
refused to answer questions, the Registrar
adjourned the examination sine die. The Offi-
cial Receiver (Mr. F. A. Dawes) intimated
that he intended to get at the bottom of the
matter.
COX, J. W., builder, of Deptford.-In this
matter the adjourned public examination took
place at the Greenwich Bankruptcy Court on
Friday week. The Registrar said the debtor
must give a deficiency account showing what
he had done with the £690 19s. 9d. he was
owing to unsecured creditors. Mr. Moss said
it had all gone on the property. The Registrar
nevertheless, adjourned the examination for
the account to be filed.
