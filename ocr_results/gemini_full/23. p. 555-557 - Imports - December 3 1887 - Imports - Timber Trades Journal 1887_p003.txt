THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases the names are published in these lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for the full amount.]
BROWN, GOODMAN, builder and contractor, Kingsland, N.—The bankrupt, who traded as Brown & Son, applied on Wednesday before Mr. Registrar Giffard to pass his public examination. The unsecured debts were stated in the accounts at £893 19s. and assets £131 10s. Upon examination the bankrupt said that he had not a partner, although he traded as Brown & Son ; he did not consider that it would mislead persons with whom he traded. He had previously failed in 1877, and again in 1882. He had no books showing his financial position. He had not a partner, although the trustees appointed under those failures had both disappeared. He resumed business in 1883 with £10 or £15, which he borrowed. He had never made out balance-sheets. If all his creditors had pressed for payment in January, 1886, he could not have paid them. He had speculated in land, but had to fight a Chancery action, which he lost, and the action cost him £300. An adjournment for accounts was ordered to the 11th January.

The debtors' costs in these actions were more than accounted for in the deficiency. After hearing the course taken for the disposal of the estate the meeting adjourned.
DOUGLAS, WILLIAM, builder and contractor, Queen's Gate, South Kensington, and Chelsea.—The debtor, who also describes himself as a furnished house proprietor, filed his petition, and on Monday a receiving order was made by Mr. Registrar Linklater. The aggregate liabilities are estimated at £675,000, of which about £575,000 are secured, and of the unsecured debts, amounting to about £100,000, the sum of £17,000 is stated to be due to trade creditors. The assets comprise equities of redemption in a large number of mortgaged houses, the free assets being roughly estimated at about £10,000, consisting chiefly of furniture in various houses which the debtor has furnished. Messrs. Mason, Phillips, & Cotton, Gresham Street, are the solicitors to the proceedings.
FINCH, JOHN BARNETT, builder and contractor, Birchington.—At the Canterbury Bankruptcy Court on Friday last the debtor came up before the Registrar for his public examination. In reply to questions by the Official Receiver, the debtor attributed his insolvency to the fact that he had not received the amount he considered owing him for building houses, and there was some question as to whether he had put them up according to instructions. The furniture belonged to his wife, who had paid for it with her own money. He had a meeting of his creditors previously, and that the Monarch Company owed him £165 7s. He filed his petition because he was pressed by Mr. Adams, a timber merchant, of Ramsgate, and by Messrs. Paramor. His wife's furniture had been sold by order of the Judge, because she was unable to deposit a certain sum in Court. In reply to a creditor, the debtor said he did not tell him a month ago that all he owed in the world was £250. The Official Receiver asked for a cash account, showing what the debtor had done with certain money received on account of the Cottage Hospital, and the examination was adjourned for a fortnight.
