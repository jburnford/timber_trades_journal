LATE GAZETTE REPORT.

GUY & BEVAN.
The first meeting of creditors was held yesterday (Friday), before Mr. Wreford, Assistant Receiver.
No offer was made, and Mr. C. F. Kemp, chartered accountant, Walbrook, was appointed trustee under the bankruptcy, together with a committee of inspection consisting of Mr. H. A. Harvey, representing the North British Bank, Mr. Wilfred Bevan, and Mr. T. Matthews, of Lamplough & Co.
The chairman said that Messrs. Edmiston & Mitchells, of Glasgow, complained that they had been returned in a trade journal as creditors for £13,000, for which there was no foundation, their firm merely appearing for that amount under the head of "Liabilities on bills other than the debtor's own," not expected to rank against the estate.
Mr. Irvine said that he made the same complaint on behalf of Messrs. Simson & Mason (Limited), who were erroneously returned as creditors for £7,500, and he was glad that notice had been taken of the subject.
The chairman intimated that the chief Official Receiver would cause further inquiries to be made.
The following observations have just been issued by the Official Receiver with reference to this failure:—
The statement of affairs is also made subject to the following special note or reservation, viz.: "That the statement is made out on the supposition that Geo. Guy & Co. and Stewart Bros. were independent firms; we, however, allege and contend that from the 1st February, 1882, the business of Geo. Guy & Co., and Guy, Bevan, & Co., was the property of Stewart Bros., and the question involved in this contention we submit to the judgment of the Court."

BIRD, A. J., furniture manufacturer and timber merchant, Hoxton.—The debtor, trading as Wainwright & Co., has filed his petition and on Thursday a receiving order was made by Mr. Registrar Giffard.
The liabilities are expected to be about £1,300.
