THE GAZETTE.
FAILURES AND ARRANGEMENTS.
[It must not be overlooked that in many cases creditors whose names are published in these lists hold security for their debts, sometimes for the full amount.]
UPTON & SON.—The statement of affairs filed by Reuben Upton and James Upton, trading in co-partnership under the style or firm of Upton & Son, contractors, of Percy Road, Ore, against whom a receiving order was recently made, discloses gross liabilities £9,612 16s. 3d., of which £9,157 17s. 3d. is fully secured and £394 19s. 5d. unsecured. The estimated value of securities is £10,770 16s. 9d., leaving a surplus to contra of £1,612 19s. 6d., and assets, estimated to realize £80, stock-in-trade £27, estimated to produce nil, and book debts £27, estimated to realize £80, household furniture £25, making a total of £1,692 19s. 6d.; deduct amount due to preferential creditors for rent, rates, and taxes £60, leaving net assets £1,632 19s. 6d., with a surplus of £1,238 0s. 1d. At the first meeting of creditors held under the presidency of Mr. A. S. Oully, Deputy Official Receiver, it was decided to accept a composition of 2s. 6d. in the pound, payable within seven days of the sanction of the Court being received, and such composition to be secured by Mr. P. E. Sankey, solicitor, of Ashford. It was considered by the creditors that the properties, some of which are unfinished, would realize little if any surplus to the estate. The public examination is at the sitting for the public examination of the Hastings Bankruptcy Court on Monday, the 7th inst. The following are the creditors :—
