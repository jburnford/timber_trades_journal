TRADE ITEMS.
MESSRS. J. P. HEIESTAD & Co., of Christiania, have removed their offices to 80, Gracechurch Street, E.C.

MR. CHARLES POWIS, engineer, has removed his offices to 1, Gracechurch Street, E.C.

GT. YARMOUTH.—Mr. James Ward will sell, without reserve, a cargo of deals, battens, and lathwood ex Sirene, at Messrs. Jewson's yard, Southtown, on Monday, the 2nd prox. The occasion of the sale is a dispute as to the goods.

WISBECH.—It is stated that the timber merchants both here and at Sutton Bridge have advanced their prices for most descriptions of timber in view of possible political complications of a warlike character in which this country may be involved.

CANADA COMPANY.—At the meeting of this company, held on the 29th inst., a dividend was declared by the directors of £1 10s. per share for the half-year ending the 10th prox. The dividends paid for the two preceding years were £2 per share each.

TENDERS.—Mr. W. Wood was the lowest tender for the supply of fagots at 16s. per 100 at the last meeting of the St. Albans Guardians of the Poor, whilst the Luton Guardians at their last meeting decided to give the contract for fagots to Mr. J. Cumberland, of Luton for fagots at 15s per 100.

H. G. GOODDAY & Co., of Paris, have removed their offices to 25, Rue des Mathurins, where they are admirably situated for the convenience of their customers. This firm, established nearly sixteen years, continues to represent some of the leading marks from Sweden and Russia. Mr. H. G. Goodday's London office remains as before, 5, Great Winchester Street, E.C.

A WOOD MERCHANT SUED.—At the last sitting of the Bromley Court, a wood merchant appeared on a judgment summons to show cause why he should not pay a sum of £1 10s. to Mr. H. J. Lukey. Mr. Weller, who appeared for Mr. Lukey, said the debtor was a well-known wood merchant in the neighbourhood, and was well able to pay £1 10s., the small amount they sought. After examining the defendant on his means, his Honour made a fresh order for payment at the rate of 10s. per month.
