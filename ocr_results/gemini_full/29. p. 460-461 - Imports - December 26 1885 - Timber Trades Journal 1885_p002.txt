ALBAN BOOTH HARRIS, cabinet-maker, of Scarborough, Knoll Lorne Park, and Lansdowne Road, Bournemouth. The first meeting of creditors herein was held at the office of the Official Receiver (Mr. F. Ashton Dawes), Salisbury, on Monday. Mr. Druitt, solicitor, attended for the debtor. The summary of the debtor's statement of affairs filed discloses liabilities to unsecured creditors £3,365 11s. 4d., creditors fully secured £50, estimated value of securities £135, surplus to contra £85, other liabilities £25, of which it is expected £25 will rank for dividend, total £3,390 11s. 4d., and assets, stock-in-trade, cost £2,451 6s. 9d., estimated to realize £1,634 4s. 6d., book debts £277 18s. 11d., estimated to produce £256 2s. 3d., household furniture £500, other property, estimated to produce £231 18s. 5d., surplus for securities in the hands of creditors holding securities £85, total £2,707 5s. 2d., less amount due to preferential creditors for rent, rates, and taxes, £182 14s. 6d., leaving nett assets £2,524 10s. 8d., to a deficiency of £866 0s. 8d., which the debtor accounts for principally by the difference between cost and present value of stock-in-trade and plant. It was resolved to accept a scheme of arrangement under which the estate will be assigned to Mr. John Hodges, the principal creditor, on withdrawing his claim, pay a dividend of 15s. in the pound to the remainder of the creditors, such composition to be payable in two equal instalments of 7s. 6d. each, at two and four months, for the sanctioning of the scheme by the Court. All preferential claims and costs also to be paid. Mr. Dawes to distribute the composition at a remuneration of 2½ per cent. The following are the creditors:—

THOMAS JORDAN, builder, Dorchester, Oxfordshire.—A receiving order was made in this case on the 7th inst. The statement of affairs shows unsecured liabilities amounting to £485 8s. 6d., against which there are assets, cash in hand £3 19s. 6d., book debts £6 17s. 6d., stock-in-trade £10, furniture £76 10s. 6d.; surplus from securities held by fully secured creditors £94, making a total of £191 7s. 6d., which after payment of preferential claims £13, leaves £178 7s. 6d. for distribution, and creates a deficiency of £309 1s. The debtor commenced business only about three years ago, and puts down the causes of his failure as ill-building contracts, and speculation in trade. The public examination is fixed for the 7th of January. The following are the principal creditors:—
