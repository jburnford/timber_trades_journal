(V 26, 1877.)
THE TIMBER TRADES JOURNAL.
43

CARDIFF.
(From Messrs. Tellefsen, Wills, and Co.'s Circular, May 25th.)
We have very little change to report this month. Arrivals have not been nearly so heavy, as shippers have been holding back on account of the lowness of the prices obtainable. So far this has made very little impression upon prices, but at the time of going to press the market seems inclined to be firmer, and we expect to see prices steadily advance from five to ten per cent. ; but we see no prospect of their speedily reaching the high figures obtainable six months since for some time, in consequence of the large stocks held by some of our principal collieries.

BARROW-IN-FURNESS.
(From our own Correspondent.)
May 23rd, 1877.
Stocks here had been pretty well reduced previous to late arrivals. These are chiefly from the Baltic ports, and will mostly go direct into consumption. Local trade, however, shows little animation.
