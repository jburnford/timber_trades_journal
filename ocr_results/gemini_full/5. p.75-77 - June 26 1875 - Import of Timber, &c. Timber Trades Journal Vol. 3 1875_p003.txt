Our contemporary the *Lumberman's Gazette* of May 29th appeared with a four-page supplement, containing amongst other news an exhaustive review of the Canadian lumber, from the pen of Mr. B. Wait. The total supply to be obtained from the Ontario forests is given at 45,550,000,000 feet broad measure; from the forests not yet in Quebec allotted limits 65,206,080,000 feet; from the Quebec allotted 298,000,000,000 making an aggregate of 408,756,080,000.
RANDALLS PATENT SYPHON CORK.—Messrs. Randall Brothers, oil merchants of 12, Lime Street, London, have just patented an ingenious little apparatus to replace the ordinary cork or stopper in a can or bottle, enabling the contents to be poured out readily without spilling, and avoiding the necessity of a funnel. Beside the tube for pouring the liquid an air-tube allows an unobstructed passage of air into the vessel, above the level of the liquid, to fill up the space caused by the liquid passing out. The syphon cork is applicable to every purpose where liquid is passed from one vessel to another, such as filling lamps, bottling wine, or lubricating engines and machinery. The convenience of this little invention far exceeds its cost, and when once used will become a necessity.
