OPEN CONTRACTS FOR WOOD.
WOOD PAVING.—For the removal of about 170 square yards of stone paving, and for replacing it by the best wood paving, 3 in deep, with 6 in. concrete foundation. Apply to Mr. D. Walker, King's College, London, W.C.
TIMBER.—The Guardians of Chesterton Union will, on 18th inst., receive tenders for 20 fathoms of deal ends, in pieces of not less than 12 in. long. Persons willing to tender are to send tenders (sealed up) to Mr. J. F. Symonds, clerk, Bene't Street, Cambridge, before 6 p.m. on 17th inst., with names of sureties.
DEAL ENDS AND BATTENS.—For supply of deal ends and battens, for firewood, to the Whitechapel Union. Tenders must be sent before noon on 8th inst. to the clerk to the Guardians, Baker's Row, Whitechapel.
DOCK AMALGAMATION.—At the half-yearly meeting of the East and West India Dock Company, recently held, it was resolved:— "That a committee of shareholders be appointed to co-operate and assist the directors in bringing about an amalgamation with the London and St. Katharine Dock Company." In pursuance of this resolution the following gentlemen have been appointed:—Mr. T. F. Burnaby Atkins, a very large holder of stock; Mr. Sydney George Holland, son of the Right Hon. Sir Henry T. Holland, Bart., Secretary of State for the Colonies; Mr. Charles Hay Stewart, West India merchant, a member of the Corporation of the City of London; and Mr. Alfred Lamb, well known for some years as a reformer of dock management.
