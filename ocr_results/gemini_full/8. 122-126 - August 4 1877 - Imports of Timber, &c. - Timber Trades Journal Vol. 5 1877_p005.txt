THE TIMBER TRADE AT THE CAPE.—The *Heidi* from Gothenburg, with 9,390 deals, the first cargo of the season, has proceeded to Port Elizabeth, 6¾d. per running foot, 3 x 9 being the price asked, which could not be got at Cape Town. 600 deals ex *Soteria* sold at 6d. per foot 3 x 9, and one or two other shipments ex *London* sold to arrive at same rate. 75,000 feet American pine boards and lumber, ex *Julia A. Brown*, rough pine sold at 2½d., shelving at 2¾d., clear pine at 4d. per sup. foot. Stocks of deals, though not large, are ample for our present limited wants, there being very little building going on at present, and Government not being in the market as purchasers. Sales of Oregon pine are being effected at 3s. 6d., and pitch pine logs at 4s. per cubic foot; but there is still a large quantity of both on hand unsold. The import duty on timber here is 2d. per cubic foot.—*Cape of Good Hope Commercial Report, 23rd July, 1877.*
