APRIL 14, 1877.] THE TIMBER TRADES JOURNAL. 407

LEITH.
(From our own Correspondent.)

The arrivals of wood goods in this Firth during last fortnight consist of 4 cargoes battens, 16 cargoes pit props, 1 cargo pitch pine, 370 pieces Memel wainscot, 33,715 oak staves, and 10,369 tree-nails.

Messrs. Hutchinson and Rogers will offer for sale at their yard, Junction-street, on the 17th, timber, deals, battens, &c., as per advertisement.
Messrs. Ferguson, Davidson, and Co. have a sale cut wood on the 19th at the Imperial Saw-Mills.
Messrs. W. Stewart and Co. have announced a sale of various wood goods on the 24th at the Burgh Saw-Mills.
Messrs. Thomson Nephews have a sale at South Alloa on Tuesday, the 24th.
I have nothing new to add regarding freights, but tonnage is wanted, Gefle, Kotka, and Sundswall to Leith. The fleet from Greenock to Quebec have all sailed, numbering 38.
