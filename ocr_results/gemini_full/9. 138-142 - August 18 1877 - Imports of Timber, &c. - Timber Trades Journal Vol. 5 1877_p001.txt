DUNDEE.
(From our own Correspondent.)
August 15th, 1877.
Trade continues on the whole pretty active at present, although the jute trade, on which this district so much depends, shows no signs of improvement. At recent sales prices of yellow pine have ruled from 1s. 11d. to 2s. 6d., average about 2s. 2d.; roofing battens, 4ths, 1½ in. flooring, 14d. to 15d. per yard.

SUNDERLAND.
(From our own Correspondent.)
August 15th, 1877.
Trade continues on the whole pretty active at present, although the jute trade, on which this district so much depends, shows no signs of improvement. At recent sales prices of yellow pine have ruled from 1s. 11d. to 2s. 6d., average about 2s. 2d.; roofing battens, 4ths, 1½ in. flooring, 14d. to 15d. per yard. Few lots of good red deals have been exposed this season and the Swedish timber is just come to hand, and as stocks were nil the cargoes have been eagerly taken up.

CARDIFF.
(From Messrs. Wills, & Co.'s Circular, Aug. 15th.)
We have no change to report in the mining timber market this month. Pitwood arrived in sufficient quantities to meet the demand of the district; prices remained therefore about the same. The stocks of Norwegian and Swedish Mining Timber are gradually becoming smaller, and considering the small quantity expected or now on the way, we expect prices for this wood will speedily advance.
